Installing solar panels in Western Australia has never been more affordable, thanks to generous government incentives and rebates. This guide covers everything you need to know about the financial benefits available to WA residents in 2025.

Small-scale Technology Certificates (STCs)

The primary incentive for residential solar in Australia is the Small-scale Renewable Energy Scheme (SRES), which provides STCs based on your system size and location. Here's how it works:

  • What are STCs? Think of them as tradeable certificates that represent the expected renewable energy your system will generate over its lifetime.
  • How much are they worth? Each STC is worth approximately $39-40 (as of late 2024), though this value fluctuates with market conditions.
  • Point of sale discount: Most installers handle the STC process and apply the value as an upfront discount on your system.

Example: A 6.6kW system in Perth generates approximately 93 STCs, providing an upfront discount of around $3,700 off your installation cost!

Estimated STC Values for Common System Sizes

System Size Approximate STCs Estimated Rebate Value*
5kW 70 STCs $2,800
6.6kW 93 STCs $3,700
10kW 140 STCs $5,600
13.2kW 185 STCs $7,400

*Based on STC value of $40. Actual values may vary.

Important: The STC scheme is scheduled to phase out by 2030, with rebate values reducing each year. Installing solar sooner means bigger savings!

Feed-in Tariffs (FiT)

When your solar panels produce more electricity than you're using, the excess is exported to the grid. Your electricity retailer pays you a feed-in tariff for this exported power.

Current Feed-in Tariff Rates in WA (2025)

  • Synergy (Metro Perth): 2.25 cents per kWh (Distributed Energy Buyback Scheme)
  • Peak export times (3pm-9pm): 10 cents per kWh
  • Some retailers offer: Up to 8-12 cents per kWh with competitive plans

While feed-in tariffs have decreased in recent years, the focus has shifted to self-consumption—using as much of your solar power as possible and potentially adding battery storage.

Interest-Free Loans and Payment Plans

Several options exist to help spread the cost of your solar investment:

  • Green loans: Some banks offer reduced-rate loans for sustainable home improvements
  • Installer payment plans: Many solar companies (including Clear Sky Solar) offer flexible payment options
  • Buy Now Pay Later: Options like Humm and Brighte allow you to pay off your system over time

Battery Storage Incentives

Adding battery storage to your solar system can maximise your savings by storing excess energy for use at night. While WA doesn't currently have a specific battery rebate, federal incentives may apply:

  • Batteries under 100kWh may be eligible for STCs
  • Some energy retailers offer virtual power plant (VPP) programs with credits for battery owners
  • Reduced reliance on grid power during expensive peak times

How to Maximise Your Savings

  1. Right-size your system: Install a system that matches your household's energy consumption
  2. Use power during the day: Run appliances when the sun is shining
  3. Consider batteries: Store excess energy for evening use
  4. Compare retailers: Shop around for the best feed-in tariffs
  5. Act soon: STC values decrease each year until 2030

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to apply for the STC rebate myself?

No. Your solar installer (like Clear Sky Solar) handles all the paperwork and applies the discount directly to your invoice.

Can I claim solar panels on my tax?

For residential systems, generally no. However, if you run a home business, a portion may be tax-deductible. Consult your accountant for specific advice.

Are there additional rebates for pensioners?

While there's no specific solar rebate for pensioners in WA, Synergy offers the Energy Assistance Payment for eligible customers. Contact Synergy for details.
